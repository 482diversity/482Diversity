Scoring.

We scored company diversity using an algorithm inspired by the field of Information Storage and Retrieval.

The field of Information Storage and Retrieval has created algorithms which are the building blocks of powerful systems such as Google, Netflix, and Amazon. These systems efficiently satisfy the information need of users based on some query. In the case of Google, users provide some text query and Google returns web page relevant to the query. In the case of Netflix, users provide some text query about the movie they would like to watch and Netflix returns movies relevant to the query. In the case of Amazon, users provide some text query about the product they are searching for and Amazon returns links to products relevant to the query. One popular algorithm for determining how relevant a document is to a user's query is the Term-Frequency-Inverted-Document-Frequency (tf-idf) algorithm, which inspired our diversity scoring algorithm.

The tf-idf algorithm analyzes how relevant a specific document is to a user's query relative to the rest of the document collection. Given a term from a user's query, a document, and the document collection, the tf-idf algorithm returns the term's frequency in the document, multiplied by the inverted document frequency of the term in the document collection.

The scoring algorithm we created is similar to tf-idf. It analyzes a collection of company documents such as the company diversity webpages or their CEO letters to shareholders using the diversity dictionary we previously generated and computes the diversity score for each document in the document collection. To generate the diversity score for a specific company document, it computes the tf-idf score for each phrase in the diversity dictionary on the specific company document relative to the collection of company documents. These phrase tf-idf scores are then summed together to compute the final diversity score.

Correlations

We generated correlations between different statistics using the Pearson correlation metric.

For each financial statistic displayed, we calculated its correlation between both the diversity scores we generated and the diversity scores produced by the Human Rights Campaign, which produces yearly diversity scores for Fortune 500 companies. We also calculated the correlation between the diversity scores we generated and the diversity scores the Human Rights Campaign generated. All of our correlations we calculated using the Pearson correlation metric, which is computed by taking the covariance of two datasets divided by the product of the standard deviation of each data set.
